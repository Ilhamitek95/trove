# Trove Seller Agreement

**Version v2 · Trove, Dubai, United Arab Emirates**

This agreement is between you (the **supplier**) and Trove. It is written in
plain English on purpose — this is genuinely how it works.

## 1. How selling on Trove works

Trove is not a middleman holding your money. Trove **buys your goods from you
and resells them** to its customers:

- You list your pieces on Trove with the price you want them to sell at (your
  **list price**).
- When a customer orders one of your pieces and their payment succeeds,
  **Trove purchases that piece from you at that moment**. Ownership (title) of
  the goods transfers to Trove at order confirmation.
- Trove is the **seller of record** to the customer. The customer buys from
  Trove, pays Trove, and holds Trove responsible for the sale.

## 2. What Trove pays you

- Your **purchase price** for each piece is your list price **minus a 20%
  margin** kept by Trove.
- Purchases are settled **weekly, on Tuesdays**, by bank transfer to the IBAN
  you provide — which must be an account **in your own name**.
- A purchase becomes payable after the piece is **delivered to the customer
  and the 7-day return window has closed**. Until then it shows in your
  dashboard as pending.
- The bank transfer reference will read **“Purchase of handmade goods”**
  followed by a purchase-order number. For each settlement Trove issues you a
  purchase note — the record of what Trove bought from you and what it paid.
  You do not need to invoice Trove.

## 3. Returns and refunds

- Because Trove is the seller of record, **customer refunds, returns and
  payment disputes are Trove's to handle**, at Trove's cost of administration.
- If a customer is refunded for an order, Trove's purchase of the goods in
  that order is reversed: title reverts to you, the piece is returned to you
  by courier, and the purchase amount is deducted from your next settlement.
  If that leaves your balance negative, the difference carries forward and
  nets against your future settlements — Trove will never take money back out
  of your bank account mid-cycle.

## 4. What you promise about your goods

You warrant that everything you list on Trove:

- is **handmade by you** (or your small team) and your own original work —
  not counterfeit, not a replica, and not carrying a brand you don't own or
  license;
- is **yours to sell** — no stolen, borrowed or third-party stock;
- is **safe for its intended use**, **lawful to sell** in the United Arab
  Emirates and accurately described and photographed;
- is **not** in a prohibited category: nothing ingestible and nothing applied
  to the skin — no food, beverages, cosmetics, skincare, soap, perfume,
  candles marketed for skin contact, or supplements.

## 5. Verifying who you are

Trove buys goods from you, so Trove must know who you are.

- If you sell **without a UAE trade or e-Trader license**, you provide before
  your first settlement: clear photos of the **front and back of your
  Emirates ID**, and your **home address**.
- These are stored **encrypted**, are visible only to Trove's administrator,
  and are used only to verify your identity, to meet Trove's legal
  obligations, and to respond to lawful requests from UAE authorities. They
  are kept only as long as Trove is required to keep them.
- Everything you provide — identity, address, bank details — must be **your
  own, current and truthful**. Selling under someone else's identity ends
  this agreement immediately.
- If your sales grow past Trove's published threshold, you will be asked to
  obtain a **UAE e-Trader or trade license** to keep scaling. Trove will
  guide you through it; with a verified license you can move to direct
  per-sale payouts.

## 6. Your accountability

- **You are accountable for the goods you supply.** If Trove suffers a loss
  because your goods or your information broke a promise in this agreement —
  a fine, a legal claim, a product-safety recall, or a customer dispute
  caused by counterfeit, unsafe or misdescribed goods — you are responsible
  for compensating Trove for that loss.
- While Trove investigates a genuine concern about your goods or identity, it
  may **pause your shop** and **hold back the settlement of the purchases
  under investigation**. Settlements resume (or reverse, under section 3) when
  the investigation closes.
- You will answer Trove's **reasonable questions** about your goods and
  identity when UAE law or a marketplace obligation requires it.
- This section works both ways in spirit: nothing in it makes you responsible
  for things Trove gets wrong — Trove remains responsible to its customers as
  the seller of record, and to you under this agreement.

## 7. Practical points

- Trove's courier collects sold pieces from you; you'll see pickup details on
  each order in your dashboard.
- Trove may update this agreement; you'll be asked to accept a new version
  before it applies to your future sales.
- Either side can end this agreement at any time; purchases already made are
  settled normally under it.
